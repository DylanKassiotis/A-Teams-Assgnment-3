Group Reflection

Dylan Kassiotis - S3856097
Project Manager & Project Designer | The A Team

A strength that the A team had was the ability to complete a large quantity of work on a short schedule. Also, the vast range of skills really allowed the team members to use their strengths such as Mignone with reviewing, Sarah with writing, formatting and reviewing and Benjamin with the creativity of the project idea and writing. On the other hand, the A team faced challenges such as inactive teammates, people joining late, late responses and not knowing if a group member is working on their objectives. For this reason, I believe in future, we would need to implement better and quicker communication tools and Activity monitoring systems to see what a group member is currently working on. There were many surprises such as, Sarah, who helped tremendously with writing and formatting, Benjamin who was vital to the project’s implementation and Mignone who also helped with some writing and reviewing. In conclusion, there were many surprises, negatives and positives with being in a group environment. However, one thing I have learned is that communication and structured project management is vital for a team.

Working with the team has been a pleasing experience. At times we lacked cohesiveness with our expectations of each other and this caused a bit of turbulence, but this can be attributed to psychological differences and is something any team is likely to encounter. On the other hand, it was a relief to have cooperative teammates willing to make sacrifices for the sake of the project. It was surprising and unfortunate to have a group member leave, and this put a greater strain on the rest of us to deliver a product consistent with our original expectations. However, I’m sure most of us learnt that the path to success lies within the human spirit, I.e. when we strive to do our best regardless of the situation at hand.
